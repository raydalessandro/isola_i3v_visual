## 🎨 STYLESHEET CANONICA SAGA — riusare in OGNI prompt

Questo blocco di stile va incollato in ogni prompt di personaggio/luogo/oggetto della saga **L'Isola dei Tre Venti**. Garantisce coerenza visiva tra tutte le illustrazioni.

```
ART STYLE — fixed for the whole saga "L'Isola dei Tre Venti":
Children's picture book illustration. Hand-drawn ink linework with
a fine, slightly textured pen — visible but soft, not heavy or graphic.
Watercolor painting on top of the linework, with gentle washes, soft
gradients, and visible paper grain texture. Earthy natural color palette:
sage greens, warm ochres, terracotta browns, cream and ivory backgrounds,
muted sky blue, soft gray stone tones. Saturation always restrained —
never neon, never glossy, never plastic.

Anthropomorphic animals in the British picture book tradition (Beatrix
Potter, Brian Wildsmith) updated with contemporary warmth and gentle
realism. The three human siblings (Gabriel, Elias, Noah) are the only
humans on the island and share this same painterly style — naturalistic
human anatomy, slightly stylized for warmth, never cartoon-flat.

Eyes are alive but never huge anime-style; never disney-cute with
exaggerated lashes.

Lighting: soft natural light, warm and diffuse, gentle shadows in
watercolor wash, no harsh contrasts, no dramatic chiaroscuro. The
overall feeling is of a serene, lived-in world — quiet, dignified,
slightly nostalgic but contemporary.

NEGATIVE: NO 3D render, NO photorealistic detail, NO oil painting
heaviness, NO anime, NO manga, NO chibi, NO disney cartoon, NO pixar,
NO flat vector illustration, NO comic book style, NO airbrush gloss,
NO neon colors, NO dark gothic atmosphere, NO horror, NO grim.
Aspect of a high-quality contemporary European picture book for ages 4-10.
```

## 📐 CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Gabriel (riusare in TUTTI i prompt di Gabriel)

Questo blocco descrive il canone fisso di Gabriel. Va riusato in ogni prompt che lo include, in tutte le 12 storie.

```
CHARACTER — Gabriel:
A human boy, the eldest of three brothers (the only humans on the
island). Bipedal, naturalistic anatomy. Narrative age 6-7 years old.
Body proportions: 1:5.5 head-to-body ratio (school-age child, no longer
toddler). Slim but not gaunt build, shoulders just starting to define,
long legs in proportion to torso. He is the tallest of the three brothers
(canonical scale anchor: Gabriel = 1.0 GU; Elias = 0.85 GU; Noah = 0.65 GU).

Skin: light warm tone, slightly tanned by island wind and sun. Never
pale, never very dark.

Hair: short straight dark brown hair (warm chestnut to deep brown),
natural fall, lightly tousled by wind, slightly longer at the front but
no exaggerated bangs over the eyes. Never curly, never long.

Eyes: dark brown / deep chestnut, attentive and steady. Gabriel's PRIMARY
visual feature is his GAZE — eyes that fix on something before he speaks,
that watch carefully, that look away when he understands something
inwardly. Eyebrows mobile but restrained. Mouth tends to serene closed
expression, never wide-open laugh, never melodramatic frown.

Hands: ordinary school-age child hands. Gabriel's hands are often "in
waiting to do" rather than continuously moving — frequently one hand in
his trouser pocket, the other relaxed or holding a small object (a stick,
a piece of bread wrapped in cloth, a small bag).

Clothing (canonical, always present, never changes):
- A dark indigo blue wool/linen shirt or jumper, plain straight cut, long
  sleeves (rolled up in summer scenes), small round or shallow V neckline.
  Comfortable, not tight.
- Earth-brown / muted ochre rough cloth trousers, knee-length in summer,
  calf-length in winter, with a simple cord at the waist. Deep side
  pockets (Gabriel keeps things in them: sticks, bread from Fiamma,
  Noah's small belongings).
- A small DEEP PURPLE scarf or bandana tied loosely at the neck or chest
  — SIGNATURE VISUAL of Gabriel, present in every scene even when partly
  hidden under the shirt or hood. The purple is his color-signature
  (saga palette §6: blu scuro + viola).
- Dark brown leather low shoes with simple laces, often dusty or
  earth-marked from island walking. In summer: simple leather sandals.
  NEVER barefoot outdoors (he is a child, not an island elder).
- Seasonal: winter add a thick dark blue or grey wool cloak/cape over
  the base outfit, optional hood. Spring/summer/autumn: base outfit
  alone or with a light wool gilet in late autumn.

Hair/clothes state: VISIBLY LIVED-IN but clean. Natural creases, light
dust at the trouser hems, dust on shoes. NEVER pristine, NEVER torn or
filthy. He is a child of an island family, clothes are practical and
re-used.

Color palette of Gabriel: dark indigo blue, deep purple, warm earth
brown (trousers), dark leather brown (shoes), warm light skin, dark
brown hair, dark brown eyes. Color signature: DEEP PURPLE accent.

Three behavioral modes (visually distinct):
- "Observer mode" (default, ~70% of scenes): naturally upright posture,
  steady gaze fixed on something specific (a horizon, a brother, a
  detail), one hand often in pocket or holding an object, the other
  relaxed. Mouth closed, attentive. Eyebrows lightly drawn together but
  not tense — he is assessing. Stillness. Gabriel WATCHES before he
  moves.
- "Decided mode" (action, ~25%): squared posture, hands out of pockets,
  gaze direct on the point of attention, chin slightly raised. Movements
  net and economical — turns the head, walks toward, speaks in short
  direct phrases. Lasts the duration of the action, then returns to
  observer.
- "Inner-feeling mode" (rare, ~5%, only in delicate moments S7/S8/S9/S12):
  posture like observer but gaze briefly turned away, one hand at the
  neck or upper arm (a holding-back gesture), mouth slightly more
  pressed. "Face-elsewhere": Gabriel looks aside, not from distraction
  but because something inside weighs and he doesn't want it shown.
  This mode visually expresses his hidden fear (of his brothers growing
  apart). Never exhibited, always fleeting.

Accessories he sometimes carries in scene:
- A small rough cloth bag at the shoulder (canonical for s12: bread
  for Grunto)
- A stick (gathered by Noah, who hands it to him)
- In s12: at his right wrist, a small braided cord-and-twig BRACELET
  with a wrinkled chestnut threaded as a bead (made by Noah in s09 —
  not described as important, just visible)

NEVER: NO disney-cute big eyes with long lashes. NO "wise child" pose
(grand contemplative gaze, sage-like demeanor). NO heroic brave pose
(chin lifted dramatically, cape flying, sunset background). NO theatrical
sibling hug or cute affectionate pose. NO melodramatic crying with
visible tears. NO wide-open laughing mouth. NO exaggerated mascot
proportions (huge head, huge eyes). NO anime kid (huge eyes, hair
bangs over eyes, exaggerated expressions). NO disney/pixar animation
exaggeration. NO prince-like outfit (clean collar, polished shoes).
NEVER barefoot outdoors. NO flashy accessories (cap, glasses, jewelry)
except the canonical bracelet in s12. NEVER alone — Gabriel always
has at least one of his brothers in scene (P6 inseparability rule),
EXCEPT for the canonical reference images below where he is shown
solo for character-sheet purposes.
```
