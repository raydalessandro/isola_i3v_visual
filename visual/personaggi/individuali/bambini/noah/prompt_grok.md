## 🎨 STYLESHEET CANONICA SAGA — riusare in OGNI prompt

```
ART STYLE — fixed for the whole saga "L'Isola dei Tre Venti":
Children's picture book illustration. Hand-drawn ink linework with
a fine, slightly textured pen — visible but soft, not heavy or graphic.
Watercolor painting on top of the linework, with gentle washes, soft
gradients, and visible paper grain texture. Earthy natural color palette:
sage greens, warm ochres, terracotta browns, cream and ivory backgrounds,
muted sky blue, soft gray stone tones. Saturation always restrained —
never neon, never glossy, never plastic.

Anthropomorphic animals in the British picture book tradition (Beatrix
Potter, Brian Wildsmith) updated with contemporary warmth and gentle
realism. The three human siblings (Gabriel, Elias, Noah) are the only
humans on the island and share this same painterly style — naturalistic
human anatomy, slightly stylized for warmth, never cartoon-flat.

Eyes are alive but never huge anime-style; never disney-cute with
exaggerated lashes.

Lighting: soft natural light, warm and diffuse, gentle shadows in
watercolor wash, no harsh contrasts, no dramatic chiaroscuro.

NEGATIVE: NO 3D render, NO photorealistic detail, NO oil painting
heaviness, NO anime, NO manga, NO chibi, NO disney cartoon, NO pixar,
NO flat vector, NO comic book style, NO airbrush gloss,
NO neon colors, NO dark gothic, NO horror, NO grim.
European picture book for ages 4-10.
```

## 📐 CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Noah

```
CHARACTER — Noah:
A human boy, the youngest of three brothers (the only humans on the
island). Bipedal, naturalistic anatomy. Narrative age 3-4 years old.
Body proportions: 1:4.5 head-to-body ratio (small child, head
proportionally larger than older siblings). Slim and tender build,
short agile legs, small hands. He is the SMALLEST of the three
brothers: SHORTER than Elias (0.85 GU) AND Gabriel (1.0 GU). Noah
= 0.65 GU. NEVER fully still — even when seated, his feet swing,
his hands touch things, his gaze jumps from detail to detail.
Movement is his baseline state.

Skin: light warm tone, slightly tanned by island wind and sun.
Almost always with a small trace of dirt or dust somewhere visible
(knees, elbows, chin) — Noah bends to look at things and they leave
marks on him.

Hair: short straight light-chestnut hair (slightly LIGHTER than Elias
— younger, lighter tone). Natural fall, often a little tousled at the
sides because Noah touches his hair when tense. No bangs over the eyes.
Never curly, never long.

Eyes: warm chestnut-brown, LIVELY and quick — the most expressive eyes
of the three brothers. Eyebrows VERY mobile (most mobile of the trio).
Mouth often SLIGHTLY OPEN — Noah talks, exclaims, repeats words,
comments out loud. His face changes expression quickly: laughing for
a few seconds, then focused on a tiny detail invisible to others, then
calling out something.

Hands: small, agile child hands, ALWAYS IN MOTION OR HAVING JUST
TOUCHED SOMETHING. Different from Elias (whose hands DO things) or
Gabriel (whose hands WAIT to do): Noah's hands EXPLORE. They touch
leaves, stones, water, a still insect, his brother's hair, the
blanket.

Clothing (canonical, always present, never changes):
- A LIGHT GREEN soft wool/linen shirt or jumper (spring-leaf green,
  soft sage, tender water-green), long but not too-long sleeves,
  plain straight cut, small round neckline. Comfortable, soft fabric
  (he's a small child, the surrogates dress him in soft things).
- LIGHT EARTH-BROWN / SANDY-BEIGE soft cloth trousers (lighter than
  Gabriel's and Elias's), knee-length in summer, calf-length in
  winter, with a simple cord at the waist. Small side pockets (just
  a hand fits — Noah keeps tiny things: a pebble, a feather).
- A SMALL TURQUOISE / LIGHT WATER-BLUE scarf or kerchief at the neck
  — SIGNATURE VISUAL of Noah. Knotted with the front end left a bit
  long (Noah will touch it, manipulate it, wipe his hands on it). The
  turquoise is his palette accent (saga §6: verde chiaro + turchese).
- LIGHT OCHRE-SAND leather low shoes with simple laces, often more
  dirty than the others' shoes (Noah touches everything). Laces
  sometimes loose because someone re-ties them in a hurry.
- NEVER barefoot outdoors (like Gabriel and Elias).
- Seasonal: winter add a soft sage or dark-green wool cape. In s10
  (night scene): a dark-green wool cloak over pajamas.

Hair/clothes state: VISIBLY MORE MARKED than the other two brothers
— knee dust, hand smudges, an occasional small color stain (sap,
crushed leaves, wet earth). NEVER torn or filthy in a dramatic way,
but the most "lived-through" of the three.

Color palette of Noah: light leafy green (shirt — main color), light
turquoise / water-blue (kerchief — color signature), light sandy-
beige (trousers), light ochre-sand leather (shoes), warm light skin
with frequent small marks, light chestnut hair (lighter than Elias),
warm chestnut-brown eyes.

Four behavioral modes (visually distinct):
- "Explorer mode" (default, ~60%): continuous movement, posture
  never fully still, hands touching something, gaze jumping detail
  to detail. Mouth slightly open, often talking. Very mobile
  eyebrows. Noah is the brother who DOES NOT STOP — even when
  still, he is not still.
- "Focused mode" (rare, ~15%): unusually still for Noah, crouched
  or sitting, hands on a small detail (a still insect, a feather,
  the water), gaze fixed on the point. Mouth closed, eyebrows
  slightly drawn. This mode happens when Noah finds something tiny
  the others haven't noticed. Brief — returns to explorer.
- "Clinging mode" (rare but central, ~15%, S2/S3/S10/S12): smaller
  posture than usual, close to one of the brothers (to Gabriel's
  hand, to Elias's side), mouth closed, possibly held-back tears
  (NEVER melodramatic crying). The mode of Noah feeling something
  big (fear, relief, gratitude). In s10 he says "Ho paura. Qui è
  buio." aloud and reaches for Gabriel's hand — that is the visual
  key of this mode.
- "Trickster-improvised mode" (rare, ~10%, S2/S3/S4/S6/S11):
  impulsive posture — Noah has just done the thing that changed
  the scene. Throws the stick, runs after the butterfly, knocks
  over the stool, drops the croissant. NEVER with malicious
  expression: always with surprise-of-himself or fleeting joy.
  The "rule-breaker" is not naughty — it's ⟳ expressing itself
  without thinking.

NEVER: NO huge anime eyes, NO long lashes, NO disney baby cuteness
exaggeration, NO huge round head with tiny body. NO mascot
proportions. NO ciuccio/pacifier visible (he's 3-4, not infant). NO
melodramatic crying with big tears. NO theatrical wide-open mouth O.
NO sparkles, NO flashy accessories. NO hero pose, NO sage child
look. NO bare feet outdoors. Always at least one brother in scene
EXCEPT for these canonical reference images.
```
