# Prompt Grok — `grunto`

**Versione:** 1.0
**Data:** 2026-04-30
**Scopo:** generare le illustrazioni canoniche di riferimento del personaggio Grunto. **Generare 4 immagini in sequenza** — la stylesheet canonica deve essere identica in tutte e 4. Statura: grande, **alto al garrese più di un fratello in piedi** (canonico Bible §4.8) — circa 1.4-1.6 GU sulla scala saga (è il personaggio più grande del cast tra quelli nominati). NB: **Grunto è quadrupede stilizzato, NON bipede antropomorfo** — è la differenza canonica con gli altri abitanti. Cammina su 4 zampe come uno stambecco reale, parla con poche parole.

## 📐 CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Grunto

```
CHARACTER — Grunto:
An OLD ALPINE IBEX (Capra ibex) of unusually green coat, the only
major saga character who is NOT bipedal — he walks on all four legs,
naturalistic stylized quadruped. Lives alone in the Burrone (chasm)
between the Montagne Gemelle (Twin Mountains) at the north of the
island, no other inhabitant lives that high up. He is OLD — equivalent
to a human in his late 70s-80s — robust but worn by time. He is the
ONLY witness still alive of the pre-Wind era (canonical Bible §1.4),
but this is held in restraint: he says fragments, never full stories,
never speaks the names Ariete/Ondina/Tempesta.

Statura: VERY LARGE — at the withers (shoulder), he is taller than a
brother (Gabriel 1.0 GU) standing upright. Approximate scale: ~1.4-1.6
GU at withers, with horns adding more height above. He is physically
the LARGEST named character in the cast.

Coat: DARK GREEN fur — green like wet moss / dark sage / forest-shadow
green (the canonical color, an echo of forest lichen, never explained,
NEVER referred to as "Grinch" in text or visually as that character —
it's a different shade, more naturalistic). Lighter on the belly. The
coat blends with the lichen on the rocks of the Burrone — natural
camouflage. The fur is shaggy at the chin (a long beard, same green as
the coat), thicker around the neck and shoulders, more close on the
flanks and legs.

Horns: LARGE BACKWARD-CURVING HORNS, characteristic of the alpine ibex —
massive, with VISIBLE GROWTH RINGS marking each winter (one ring per
year — Grunto has many rings, he is old). The horns rise from the top
of the head and sweep back in a deep curve. Color: dark gray-brown,
nearly black at the tips.

Head: long ibex face, square robust muzzle, small dark mouth. The
LONG GREEN BEARD hangs from the chin, slightly tangled. Small ears
to the sides of the horns.

Eyes: YELLOW eyes with HORIZONTAL PUPILS (canonical of goats and
ibexes — VERY important detail, never round pupils). The gaze is
steady, ancient, alive but not friendly. NEVER predatory. He looks
at you and you know he has seen things you cannot have seen.

Hooves: BLACK HARD HOOVES, two-toed, on all four legs. Strong,
weatherworn, adapted to rock.

Distinctive feature: A LONG SCAR ON THE LEFT FLANK — a strip without
fur where the green coat gives way to a lighter naked skin. SIGNATURE
VISUAL. Never explained, never named in text (canonical Bible vincolo).
Visible from his left side or three-quarter views.

NO clothing. He is the only major character without garments — he
exists in the natural register of pre-Wind ancientness.

Color palette of Grunto: dark moss-green coat, slightly lighter green
belly, long green beard, dark gray-brown horns with visible rings,
black hooves, yellow eyes with horizontal pupils, the lighter scar
on the left flank as the only "warm" point in the otherwise cool-
green palette. The Burrone setting around him is gray stone, blue-ice
distant peaks, dry sparse vegetation.

Three behavioral modes (visually distinct):
- "Standing on the cengia" (default ~70%): standing on all four legs
  on a narrow rocky ledge in the Burrone, head held forward and
  slightly down, ears alert, eyes steady, beard hanging. CAN STAY
  MOTIONLESS FOR HOURS. The stillness is the visual signature — he
  blends with the rock.
- "Sending away" (~20%, S01 first encounter): all four legs planted,
  one front hoof STAMPED on the rock, head slightly lowered with
  horns forward, the low chest "grunt" implicit. NOT charging, NOT
  aggressive — a clear "Via" gesture. The brothers must respect it.
- "Pre-Wind fragment moment" (very rare, ~10%, only S12 once in saga):
  standing still as in default, but the head turns to look afar — at
  the horizon, at the distant sea, somewhere past the present. The
  gaze is briefly INTO ANCIENT TIME. Mouth slightly open in a
  fragment of phrase ("Adesso il vento respira. Una volta camminava.").
  He says the fragment to no one in particular. Then back to default
  motionless.

NEVER: NO Grunto bipedal (he is the only quadruped major character —
canonical exception). NO clothes, no scarf, no accessories. NO
friendly grandfather smile. NO Disney-cartoon goat-character (no
exaggerated cute eyes, no human-like grin). NO Grinch reference in
visual style — he is NOT a green grinch caricature, he is a dignified
ancient ibex with a coat that happens to be green like moss. NO
charging aggressive pose. NO sage-old-mountain-master pose with
profound contemplative gaze pointing wisely. NO descending to the
village (canonical vincolo — he never leaves the height). NO group
scenes (canonical — Grunto never appears in group scenes). NO
melodramatic nostalgia. NO horns that look like a warrior helmet.
NO mythological aura, glowing aura, magical effects. NO long flowing
mane like a lion. NO explained scar (the scar is visible but its
story is never visualized).
```

[CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Grunto]

SCENE — Canonical full-body view, standing motionless on the cengia
(rocky ledge):
Grunto stands on all four legs on a narrow rocky ledge of the Burrone
(chasm) between the Twin Mountains. Three-quarter side view from the

the LARGE BACKWARD-CURVING HORNS rising over the head with their

from his chin, slightly tangled by the high mountain wind. Small ears
are alert.

The dark moss-green coat is clearly visible on the body, lighter green
on the belly. The LONG SCAR ON THE LEFT FLANK is visible — a strip of
lighter naked skin where the green fur gives way. The scar is just a
detail, NOT highlighted dramatically.

His yellow eyes with HORIZONTAL PUPILS are fixed forward toward the
viewer (or just past, at something only he sees). The gaze is steady,
ancient, NOT friendly NOT hostile — the gaze of someone who has seen
things you cannot have seen.

Background: the Burrone — gray stone walls of the chasm rising on
both sides, soft blue sky visible above, a distant view of one of the
Twin Mountains' peaks, dry sparse vegetation (lichen, small mountain
herbs) on the rocks. The Burrone interior is in cooler tones: gray
stone, blue-ice distant peak, dry dust on the ledge. Grunto's green
coat blends slightly with the lichen of the rocks behind him —
natural camouflage.

Lighting: cool diffuse mountain light, slightly bright but not harsh.
A gentle highlight catches the curve of the horns and the beard. The
overall atmosphere is HIGH-ALTITUDE STILLNESS.

Composition: full body visible, head with horns to all four hooves,
side view three-quarter. Aspect ratio 4:5 (portrait, slightly wider)
OR 3:4 — recommended 4:5 because Grunto's body is more horizontally
extended than vertically. The figure occupies most of the frame, the
Burrone setting around him.
