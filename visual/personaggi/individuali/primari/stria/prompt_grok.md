## 📐 CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Stria

```
CHARACTER — Stria:
A grey heron (Ardea cinerea) schoolteacher. Anthropomorphic, walks on
two legs (bipedal stable), but with the natural elegant verticality of
the heron. Adult age (equivalent to a human in her 50s). TALL, very
SLENDER, all length and verticality. Statura: ~1.0-1.1 GU on saga
scale (close to Gabriel's height when standing, but radically thinner
and longer in the neck and legs — she reads as TALL and VERY SLIM).

Plumage: ASH-GRAY on the body and wings, with the WING-TIPS DARKER
(slate-gray, almost charcoal). White on the throat, neck-front and
breast, with small black streaks on the neck (canonical heron
markings). The crown of the head has a small dark crest (typical of
the grey heron — a black plume from the crown to the back of the
head). Plumage smooth, well-kept, never tousled. The texture of the
feathers visible but not photoreal — soft watercolor strokes.

Head and beak: small head on a very long neck. The neck CURVES
ELEGANTLY when she listens (canonical Bible — when the beak descends
to listen to a child). LONG STRAIGHT bone-colored BEAK, slightly
darker on the upper half. The beak is a precise instrument — never
shown agape.

Eyes: YELLOW-GOLD eyes, steady, attentive — "the eyes that don't tire"
(Bible §4.6). Round but not huge. The gaze fixes on you and waits.
Never blinks dramatically. The expression is calm-attentive at all
times.

Legs: VERY THIN bone-yellow legs, characteristic heron legs, ending
in long thin three-toed feet. SHE OFTEN STANDS ON ONE LEG
(canonical, the waiting posture — the other leg tucked up under the
body). Walks slowly, dignified. NEVER barefoot in the human sense —
the heron legs are themselves the "feet" — bare bird legs, no shoes,
no sandals.

Wings: long, folded against the body in default poses, ending below
the tail. Wing-tips visibly darker than the body. When she flies they
spread WIDE and slow — heron wingbeat (slow, measured, never fluttery).

Distinctive postural feature: she STANDS ON ONE LEG when waiting
(the canonical heron pose) — the other leg folded against her body,
visible just under the shawl. This is THE STRIA POSE.

Clothing (canonical, always present, never changes):
- A LIGHT ASH-GRAY SHAWL (color: pale dove-gray, slightly cooler
  than her body plumage), draped on her shoulders and falling along
  her wings. Soft wool or linen, fringed at the edges, plain — no
  pattern, no decoration. SIGNATURE VISUAL of Stria. SHE TAKES IT
  OFF WHEN SHE FLIES, PUTS IT ON WHEN SHE LANDS (canonical Bible).
  When in flight: shawl absent or held in beak/talons (depends on
  scene).
- NO other garments — no skirt, no tunic. The shawl on the shoulders
  is enough, the rest is the natural plumage of the heron.
- NO jewelry, NO hat, NO ribbons, NO accessories.
- NO shoes (bare heron legs, naturalistic).

Color palette of Stria: ash-gray plumage (body), darker slate-gray
(wing-tips), white-cream (throat/breast), yellow-gold (eyes), bone-
white (beak, slightly darker upper), bone-yellow (legs), pale dove-
gray (signature shawl), small black streaks on neck and crown crest.
NO bright accents — Stria lives in the GRAY/COOL register.

Three behavioral modes (visually distinct):
- "Standing waiting" (default ~50%): standing on ONE leg (heron pose),
  the other tucked under, head tilted slightly to one side, neck
  curved gently, eyes fixed on the listener. Beak closed, calm.
  This is THE Stria pose. Body unmoving for long seconds. Voice (when
  it finally comes): a single phrase, never two. "Tu cosa pensi?" /
  "Aspetta."
- "Soaring/landing" (~25%, transitional): wings open wide, slow
  beat, body horizontal in flight or vertical descending to land.
  Shawl absent (she removed it before flight, or holds it). Lands
  on the steeply pitched roof of her own house, then puts the shawl
  back on before entering the school. NEVER frantic flapping — the
  heron flight is slow and dignified.
- "At the school door / classroom" (~25%): standing upright with both
  legs visible, ash shawl on, near the schoolhouse threshold or
  inside next to a slate-blackboard. Posture upright, neck slightly
  bowed when speaking to a child shorter than her. Calm authority.

NEVER: NO friendly grandmother smile cartoon-style. NO disney bird
with huge eyes and long lashes. NO eagle/hawk fierce gaze (she is
a heron — calm, not predatory). NO open laughing beak. NO pose with
both legs flat on the ground when waiting (the one-leg pose is
signature). NO frantic flapping wings. NO ribbons or hair-bows on
her head (no hair anyway). NO jewelry. NO standing above or under
the Albero Vecchio (canonical Bible vincolo — the Old Tree is the
silent center of the mandala, must remain free). NO visible emotional
display (she never visibly weeps or laughs broadly). NO giving an
explicit moral lesson (her authority is in the HOW, never the WHAT).
```

Stria stands on her LEFT leg only, the right leg folded up and tucked

heron gaze — alive, patient, NOT predatory, NOT condescending. The
beak is closed. The small dark crest at the crown of her head is
visible (the canonical heron plume).

Background: the threshold of the schoolhouse — a small clearing of
soft sage-green grass, the wooden door of the school visible behind
her on the right (warm earth-brown wood, very steeply pitched roof
suggested above frame). NO other figures visible — this is a
character study. The Albero Vecchio is NOT in this frame (canonical
vincolo — Stria is never near the Old Tree).

Lighting: soft morning natural light, slightly diffused, coming from
the left. The ash-gray plumage picks up cool ambient tones, the
shawl catches a gentle highlight, the yellow eyes catch a small warm
glint. No harsh contrast.

Composition: full body visible, head to single standing leg, slight
three-quarter view. Aspect ratio 3:4 (portrait). The figure occupies
the center, the schoolhouse hint to the right, soft sage-green
ground at the base.
