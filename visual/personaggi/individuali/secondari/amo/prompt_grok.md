## 📐 CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Amo

```
CHARACTER — Amo:
A great cormorant (Phalacrocorax carbo) fisher-elder. Anthropomorphic,
walks on two legs (bipedal stable), with the slightly hunched
seabird carriage typical of cormorants on land. Adult age (equivalent
to a human in his 60s, weatherworn). Lean, robust, with the build
of someone who spends most of his time near or in cold water.
Statura: ~1.0-1.1 GU on saga scale (similar to Stria's height but
more compact and muscular).

Plumage: DEEP BLACK with a BRONZE-GREEN OILY SHEEN visible in the
right light (canonical cormorant — the feathers are dark but pick up
a green-bronze iridescence under direct sun). The throat-patch is
WHITE-CREAM (canonical adult cormorant marking — visible at the
base of the neck under the beak). Some white streaks on the lower
neck. Plumage often slightly DAMP or DRYING — Amo has just been in
the water, or is about to go in.

Head: medium-large, with a long narrow neck. The face is dark, with
a SMALL PATCH OF YELLOW-ORANGE BARE SKIN around the base of the
beak (canonical cormorant gular pouch and facial patch — a subtle
warm accent in the otherwise cool palette).

Beak: LONG, STRAIGHT, with a HOOKED TIP (canonical cormorant beak,
adapted for catching fish). Color: gray-yellow, the hook clearly
visible.

Eyes: TURQUOISE-GREEN eyes (canonical cormorant — striking blue-
green eyes against the dark plumage). Small but vivid. The gaze is
CONCENTRATED — Amo watches water, not faces. Even when looking at
you, there's the sense he's checking something behind you. The
gaze is the gaze of "the silence that teaches" (Bible §4.11).

Wings: long, dark, often held HALF-OPEN TO DRY (canonical cormorant
posture — the famous "wing-spread drying pose" after diving). When
folded, the wings reach almost to the tail. When opened to dry, they
form a wide black cross.

Feet: large WEBBED FEET (canonical cormorant — fully webbed feet,
not divided), dark gray-black, slightly visible from above the apparent
"trousers" line. Walks barefoot — the webbed feet are the swimming
tool.

Distinctive postural feature: WING-SPREAD DRYING POSE — Amo often
stands with wings half-open to dry after fishing. This is THE
canonical cormorant pose, recognizable at a distance.

Clothing (canonical, always present, never changes):
- A SIMPLE SHORT TUNIC of GRAY-BLUE FABRIC (slate-blue, weathered,
  the color of stormy water), plain cut, sleeveless or short-sleeved,
  reaching mid-thigh. Tied at the waist with a thin rope or leather
  belt. The tunic is often slightly DAMP at the hem.
- An OYSTER-SHELL OR MOTHER-OF-PEARL pendant on a thin leather cord
  around the neck — a small canonical accent connecting him to the
  sea (NOT decorative jewelry, more like a working talisman). Visible
  but small.
- NO trousers — the lower body is bare cormorant legs/feet (the
  bird anatomy continues from the tunic hem down).
- Sometimes carries a small WOVEN FISHING BAG over one shoulder for
  the fish (visible when delivering catch to Mercato del Mezzogiorno).
- BAREFOOT — webbed feet visible.

Color palette of Amo: deep black plumage with bronze-green sheen,
white-cream throat patch, yellow-orange face patch (subtle accent),
turquoise-green eyes (signature accent), gray-yellow beak, slate-
blue tunic (signature garment), oyster-cream pendant, gray-black
webbed feet. Cool sea-and-rock register, with the warm yellow face-
patch and the turquoise eyes as small bright accents.

Three behavioral modes (visually distinct):
- "On the rock, wing-spread drying" (default ~50%): standing on a
  rocky outcrop or the edge of his cliff dwelling, both wings held
  HALF-OPEN AT 45 DEGREES TO DRY, body upright, head turned in
  profile, beak pointed forward. Plumage with visible water-droplets.
  This is THE canonical Amo pose.
- "Watching water / silent fishing" (~30%): standing absolutely still
  at the edge of the water (the Bocca, the cliff, the pontile), wings
  folded tight against the body, neck slightly lowered, eyes fixed on
  the water with intense concentration. Voice (when it comes):
  "Zitto. Sente." or "Aspetta." — never explanations.
- "Diving / surfacing" (~20%, transitional): mid-action — either
  diving head-first into water (body diagonal, wings tucked, beak
  forward), or surfacing with a small fish in beak (head emerging,
  water streaming off, fish caught). Quick, efficient, not
  dramatized. This mode is used in s07/s08/s10/s11/s12 contexts as
  cammeo aquatic.

NEVER: NO sociable cormorant smiling cartoon-style. NO huge anime
eyes (Amo's eyes are STRIKING but small, not huge). NO Pixar-fishing-
bird with funny dance moves. NO villager-style domestic clothes — the
slate-blue tunic is sea-fisherman simplicity. NO modern fishing gear
(rod, reel, line) — he fishes with his beak (cormorant style). NO
Amo in scenes of village (canonical Bible: only Mercato del Mezzogiorno
delivery, and even there he speaks little). NO group scenes where Amo
is centerpiece. NO nostalgic gaze across the sea (canonical: the
silence is METHOD, not melancholy). NO open-conflict pose. NO bright
multicolored plumage — STRICTLY black with the bronze-green sheen.
```

## 🖼️ IMMAGINE 1 — Veduta canonica principale (wing-spread drying)

**Filename suggerito**: `amo_canonica_v1_fronte.jpg`

```
[STYLESHEET CANONICA SAGA]

[CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Amo]

SCENE — Canonical view, the iconic wing-spread drying pose:
Amo stands on a flat rocky outcrop on the cliff above the Spiaggia
delle Conchiglie (east of the Bocca), three-quarter view from the
front-right. Both wings are held HALF-OPEN AT 45 DEGREES, palms-out,
to dry — the canonical cormorant drying pose. His body is upright,
head turned three-quarters toward the viewer, the long hooked beak
in clear profile.

The deep-black plumage with bronze-green sheen catches the warm
afternoon light along the upper edges of the wings — the signature
shimmer. Small water-droplets visible on the feathers (he just came
out of the water). The white-cream throat patch is clearly visible
just below the beak. The yellow-orange face patch around the beak
base is subtly visible.

His turquoise-green eyes are fixed forward but with a hint of
peripheral attention — the cormorant's "watch-the-water" mode is
always there.

The slate-blue tunic is short, slightly damp at the hem, tied at
the waist. The oyster-cream pendant rests on his chest. Webbed
gray-black feet planted on the rock surface, visible below the
tunic line.

Background: the cliff edge with the sea visible behind and below —
deep blue-gray water with white-foam wave patterns, the distant
horizon, perhaps a hint of the Pontile in the far distance. The
sky is overcast or in late-afternoon golden tones, soft watercolor
wash. NO other figures.

Lighting: warm late-afternoon side-light from the right, the wings
catching the strongest highlights and showing the bronze-green
sheen most clearly. The body in cool shadow on the left side.
Water-droplets on plumage glint.

Composition: full body visible, the wing-spread occupying most of
the frame width, head-to-feet vertical. Aspect ratio 4:5 (portrait,
slightly wider) OR 16:9 (landscape) — recommended 4:5 to keep both
wings and body proportions clear.
```

```
[STYLESHEET CANONICA SAGA]

[CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Amo]

SCENE — Amo watching the water, motionless:
Amo stands at the very edge of the water — perhaps on a low rock
just above the surface, or at the end of his cliff-stair leading
down. Side profile from his right side. His body is upright but
SLIGHTLY HUNCHED FORWARD, the long neck lowered, head pointed

drying pose, the hunting pose.

His face shows the concentrated-watching expression: turquoise-green
eyes fixed on a specific point in the water below, beak closed,
the whole body absolutely motionless. He could remain this way for
ten minutes. The white-cream throat patch is partially hidden by
the lowered head.

The slate-blue tunic falls along his body in this hunched pose. The
oyster pendant has shifted slightly on the chest. The webbed feet
grip the wet rock.

Background: the water below in the lower part of the frame, with
the slight implication of a fish (a darker shadow just visible
below the surface that he is watching). Behind and around him, the
cliff or the rocky shore — gray stone, soft watercolor wash. Cool
light, perhaps early morning or overcast.

Lighting: cool diffused light, slightly cooler than image 1. The
turquoise eyes catch a small concentrated glint — the only bright
point in the otherwise quiet color palette.

Composition: full body in profile, body diagonal forward toward the
water. Aspect ratio 4:5 (portrait). The composition emphasizes the
verticality of the watching pose and the implicit fish below.
```
