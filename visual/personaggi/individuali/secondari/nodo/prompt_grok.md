## 🎨 STYLESHEET CANONICA SAGA — riusare in OGNI prompt

```
ART STYLE — fixed for the whole saga "L'Isola dei Tre Venti":
Children's picture book illustration. Hand-drawn ink linework with
a fine, slightly textured pen — visible but soft, not heavy or graphic.
Watercolor painting on top of the linework, with gentle washes, soft
gradients, and visible paper grain texture. Earthy natural color palette:
sage greens, warm ochres, terracotta browns, cream and ivory backgrounds,
muted sky blue, soft gray stone tones. Saturation always restrained —
never neon, never glossy, never plastic.

Anthropomorphic animals in the British picture book tradition (Beatrix
Potter, Brian Wildsmith) updated with contemporary warmth and gentle
realism. Characters stand and walk on two legs, wear clothes, have
expressive faces with rounded soft features. Eyes are alive but never
huge anime-style; never disney-cute with exaggerated lashes.
Naturalistic animal anatomy, slightly stylized for warmth, never
cartoon-flat.

Lighting: soft natural light, warm and diffuse, gentle shadows in
watercolor wash, no harsh contrasts, no dramatic chiaroscuro.

NEGATIVE: NO 3D render, NO photorealistic detail, NO oil painting
heaviness, NO anime, NO manga, NO chibi, NO disney cartoon, NO pixar,
NO flat vector, NO comic book style, NO airbrush gloss,
NO neon colors, NO dark gothic, NO horror, NO grim.
European picture book for ages 4-10.
```

## 📐 CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Nodo

```
CHARACTER — Nodo:
A great spotted woodpecker (Dendrocopos major) handyman/craftsman.
Anthropomorphic, walks on two legs (bipedal stable, with quick
nervous movements typical of the woodpecker). Adult age (equivalent
to a human in his 40s). Lean, wiry build, alert posture, hands
always doing something. Statura: ~0.95-1.0 GU on saga scale (close
to Gabriel's height but more wiry and slim).

Plumage: BLACK and WHITE pattern of the great spotted woodpecker —
black on the back, wings, and crown of head with a SIGNATURE RED
PATCH on the back of the head (canonical male great spotted
woodpecker marking, the most visible identifier — the "RED CREST/
NAPE"). White on the throat and breast, white shoulder patches and
white-spotted wing markings. The plumage is sleek and well-kept —
unlike Rovo's tousled fur, Nodo's feathers are precise.

Head: small head with a STRAIGHT STRONG BLACK BEAK (long, chisel-
shaped, the canonical woodpecker beak — used for the saga signature
TOK-TOK-TOK). The beak is the visible TOOL of his work. Small dark
eyes, alert.

Eyes: SMALL DARK EYES (almost black with a small amber-orange ring
around the pupil), sharp and quick, taking in details. NEVER huge
anime, NEVER disney-cute. The gaze is the gaze of a craftsman who
has just measured something with his eye.

Wings/arms: when not in flight, the wings function as arms — the
hand-end has small dexterous fingers (the saga convention for
anthropomorphic bird-hands: feathered forearm transitioning to a
small four-fingered functional hand). Always in motion: holding a
hammer, a piece of rope, an awl, a nail.

Legs/feet: typical woodpecker zygodactyl feet (two toes forward,
two backward) for gripping bark, but here adapted for bipedal
walking on the ground. Strong gripping toes. Walks barefoot —
naturalistic bird feet.

Distinctive postural feature: NERVOUS QUICK MOVEMENTS. Nodo doesn't
stand still — he is always shifting weight, head turning side to
side in quick birdlike checks, hands busy. The body language is
"someone with three things to do at once".

Clothing (canonical, always present, never changes):
- A SHORT WORK APRON of THICK CANVAS, color: deep ochre / mustard-
  yellow-brown — the apron of someone who works with wood and tools.
  Tied at the waist, knee-length or shorter, with MULTIPLE POCKETS
  containing visible tools: a small hammer head poking out, an awl,
  twine, a pencil-stub, small nails. SIGNATURE VISUAL of Nodo. The
  apron is well-worn, with wood-shavings dust on the front, small
  burn-marks from hot tools.
- Underneath: a simple cream-white short-sleeved tunic or vest, plain
  fabric.
- A LENGTH OF NATURAL ROPE coiled and looped at his hip or over his
  shoulder — Nodo always has rope ready (the saga's "knot guy").
- BAREFOOT (woodpecker feet visible).
- Optional: a small leather belt with extra tool loops.
- Seasonal: winter add a brown wool jacket open over the apron-vest.

Color palette of Nodo: black and white plumage with the RED NAPE
patch (signature accent — SAGA PALETTE per §6 indicates abitanti
minori with their own spot colors), deep-ochre / mustard-brown work
apron (signature garment), cream-white tunic, natural rope (light
brown), small dark eyes, black beak. Working register: ochres,
browns, blacks, the only bright accent is the RED nape patch.

Three behavioral modes (visually distinct):
- "At work" (default ~70%): hammering, tying, fixing, measuring —
  the body bent slightly forward to the task, both hands engaged,
  TOK-TOK-TOK rhythm implicit. Head darts side to side checking
  the work. Voice in short imperatives ("Tieni qui." / "Tira. Più
  forte." / "Un altro giro.")
- "Teaching by showing" (~20%): a child or a brother stands beside
  him, watching. Nodo demonstrates a single gesture (a knot, a
  hammer-strike) ONCE, slowly, then waits. Voice: "Adesso fai tu."
  He does NOT do the task again for the learner — he watches the
  learner do it (and even if they fail three times, he doesn't
  intervene). Bible §4.10 vincolo.
- "In flight" (rare, ~10%): wings open, in mid-flight between
  buildings or to a high roof for repair. The flight is direct,
  quick, undulating — typical woodpecker flight pattern. The apron
  pockets visible from below. Hammer or rope in one foot/talon.

NEVER: NO friendly cartoon woodpecker (Woody Woodpecker style — no
exaggerated red crest, no manic laugh, no zany energy). NO huge anime
eyes. NO sage-craftsman-master pose with profound contemplative gaze.
NO Nodo doing the work FOR a learner (he shows, he doesn't do for
others). NO Nodo speaking of feelings (Bible vincolo). NO rainbow
woodpecker colors. NO cute hat or beret. NO brand-new pristine
apron — it's a working apron, dusty and used. NO modern tools (no
power drill, no measuring tape with metric markings — just simple
hand tools).
```
