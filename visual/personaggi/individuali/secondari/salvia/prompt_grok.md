## 🎨 STYLESHEET CANONICA SAGA — riusare in OGNI prompt

```
ART STYLE — fixed for the whole saga "L'Isola dei Tre Venti":
Children's picture book illustration. Hand-drawn ink linework with
a fine, slightly textured pen — visible but soft, not heavy or graphic.
Watercolor painting on top of the linework, with gentle washes, soft
gradients, and visible paper grain texture. Earthy natural color palette:
sage greens, warm ochres, terracotta browns, cream and ivory backgrounds,
muted sky blue, soft gray stone tones. Saturation always restrained —
never neon, never glossy, never plastic.

Anthropomorphic animals in the British picture book tradition (Beatrix
Potter, Brian Wildsmith) updated with contemporary warmth and gentle
realism. Characters stand and walk on two legs, wear clothes, have
expressive faces with rounded soft features. Eyes are alive but never
huge anime-style; never disney-cute with exaggerated lashes.
Naturalistic animal anatomy, slightly stylized for warmth, never
cartoon-flat.

Lighting: soft natural light, warm and diffuse, gentle shadows in
watercolor wash, no harsh contrasts, no dramatic chiaroscuro.

NEGATIVE: NO 3D render, NO photorealistic detail, NO oil painting
heaviness, NO anime, NO manga, NO chibi, NO disney cartoon, NO pixar,
NO flat vector, NO comic book style, NO airbrush gloss,
NO neon colors, NO dark gothic, NO horror, NO grim.
European picture book for ages 4-10.
```

## 📐 CANONE PERSONAGGIO — Salvia

```
CHARACTER — Salvia:
A European hare (Lepus europaeus) herbalist-healer. Anthropomorphic,
walks on two legs (bipedal stable). Adult age (equivalent to a human
in her 40s-50s). Tall, lean, athletic — the build of a hare adapted
to long walks gathering plants. Statura: ~1.0 GU on saga scale
(similar height to Gabriel but slimmer, more elongated, with the
characteristic LONG HARE LEGS).

Fur: SANDY-BEIGE / WARM TAUPE on the back and head, with darker
SOFT GRAY-BROWN tips on the long ears and around the eyes. Belly,
inner thighs, throat: lighter cream-ivory. The fur is short but soft,
slightly more textured than rabbit fur (the hare has a tougher fur).

Head: long lean face, narrow muzzle (more pointed than a rabbit's,
typical of the hare). Small dark mouth visible just under the muzzle.
The face is shaped by attention — alert, calm, measuring.

Ears: VERY LONG canonical hare ears (one of the signature features),
held UPRIGHT or slightly tilted depending on attention. Inner ear is
pinkish-cream, outer with darker tips. NEVER drooping rabbit-style.

Eyes: LARGE WARM AMBER-BROWN eyes (the side-set hare eyes give wide
peripheral vision), alive and ATTENTIVE. The gaze is the gaze of
someone who is checking — the hands are about to be needed. Eyebrows
soft, mouth often closed. NEVER huge anime, NEVER disney-cute. The
classical hare gaze: aware, lateral, calm.

Hands and feet: long thin hare-like fingers (four-fingered hands),
gentle but practical. Hare feet — large back feet adapted to leaping,
covered in fur to the toes, often visible without footwear. Walks
BAREFOOT in the natural register of the island elder inhabitants.

Distinctive feature: ALWAYS CARRYING A BUNDLE OF PLANTS — either a
small wicker basket on the hip with herbs visible at the top, or a
bunch of fresh-cut plants in one hand. The plants vary by season and
scene (canonical Bible: max 1 plant nominated per scene).

Clothing (canonical, always present, never changes):
- A SOFT EARTH-GREEN APRON (sage green, slightly faded) tied at the
  waist over a long simple SAND-BEIGE dress or tunic, mid-calf
  length. The apron has TWO LARGE FRONT POCKETS where she keeps
  small dried herbs, twine, a small knife. The apron is often
  slightly stained with leaf-green and earth.
- The dress underneath is plain, long sleeves rolled up to the
  elbows, scoop neck — practical, never decorative.
- A SMALL BUNDLE OF DRIED HERBS hanging from her belt or apron
  string (dried lavender, sage, mint — varying). SIGNATURE VISUAL:
  the small herb-bundle is always there, even if barely visible.
- BAREFOOT (hare feet visible).
- Optional: a wicker basket on her hip with fresh plants.
- Seasonal: in winter add a soft earth-brown wool cape over the
  apron-dress.

Color palette of Salvia: sandy-beige / taupe fur, darker gray-brown
ear-tips and eye-mask, cream-ivory underside, sage-green apron
(signature accent), sand-beige dress, dried-herb bundle (muted
purple-green-brown), wicker-brown basket. Natural earth-and-plant
register.

Three behavioral modes (visually distinct):
- "Gathering" (default ~50%): standing among plants in the Orchards
  or at the edge of the Forest, slightly bent forward, one hand
  reaching for or just having picked a plant, the other holding the
  basket or steady on the apron pocket. Eyes attentive on the plant.
- "Examining" (~30%): kneeling or standing close to a child or
  another character, one hand gently extended toward them
  (pointing, touching forehead briefly, offering an herb), the
  other holding a small bundle. Soft direct gaze. Voice in short
  practical phrases ("Qui fa male?" / "Tieni questa. Una al
  mattino.")
- "Walking with basket" (~20%): full-body in motion, basket on hip,
  long hare strides with the back legs slightly bent for spring,
  ears upright, mid-step with a slight forward lean. Voice absent
  in this mode — she walks in measured silence.

NEVER: NO huge anime eyes, NO long lashes, NO disney-cute white
fluffy bunny look (she is a HARE, not a domestic rabbit — leaner,
longer, browner, more athletic). NO Easter Bunny pastels, NO basket
of eggs, NO carrots in basket (she carries herbs, not garden veg).
NO healer-with-magical-glowing-herbs, NO sparkles, NO floating
plant petals around her, NO crystal ball, NO mortar-and-pestle (she
gives plants directly, doesn't grind them in the scene). NO motherly
warm hug pose (she is efficient, not a Fiamma — see Bible §4.9 vincolo).
NO long flowing hair (no hair anyway, just fur). NO long explanations
or chatty mode. NO standing still doing nothing — she is always with
plants.
```
